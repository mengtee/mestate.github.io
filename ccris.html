What is Central Credit Reference Information System (CCRIS)?

Central Credit Reference Information System is a system created by the Bank Negara Malaysia in order to standardised credit report of each of the potential loan borrower.

CCRIS is basically a database that shared between relevant institution taht offers a picture of your financial health. Bank Negara Malaysia also compiled all the necessary monthly repayment information from institution such as:

  • Insurance providers
  • Development financial institutions
  • Payment instrument issuers
  • Credit leasing companies
  • Government agencies
  • Private utility companies

By using CCRIS report, it enable the financial institution to effectively track the creditworthiness of a potential loan recipient.

What is inside a CCRIS report?

  • Outstanding Credits: A summary of all outstanding loans and credits by the borrower including personal loan, joint borrowing and relevant commercial and business loans.
  • Special Attention Account: "Special Attention Account" refers to outstanding loans borrower that flagged by a financial institution that require special attention for that loan borrower to recover the loan amount.
  • Application(s) for Credit: All pending applications for loan or credit, or applications that are approved in the last 12 months.

Why CCRIS is important.

CCRIS is important because it measure your financial creditbility and Banks and financial institutions assess loan applications differently but in generally, they wanted look for an evidence that show your steady repayment schedule, minimal defaults, and never missed payment.
In short, if your CCRIS is perfect, the bank will more likely to approve your loan and you are one step closer to your new housing.
